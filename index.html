Beyond the raw numbers, heroes fit into specific roles within the meta. This section breaks down the top heroes into qualitative categories to explain *why* they were so dominant or prioritized.

The Power Picks

Heroes with both a high pick rate and a high win rate. These are reliable, strong, and consistent choices that formed the core of many team compositions.

Claude (Gold Lane)

With a 62.9% WR over 62 games, Claude was the premier marksman. His high mobility with Battle Mirror Image and massive teamfight damage from Blazing Duet made him a late-game monster that was difficult to lock down.

Baxia (Jungle)

A 63.2% WR in 38 games. Baxia's value came from his fast rotations with Baxia-Shield Unity, durable nature, and built-in anti-heal from his passive. He excelled at disrupting the enemy backline and controlling objectives.

Nolan (Jungle)

The most picked hero (88 games) with a solid 54.5% WR. Nolan's exceptional mobility, fast jungle clear, and high burst damage made him the default assassin. He could get in, eliminate a target, and get out with ease.

The Priority Bans

Heroes with overwhelmingly high ban rates. Teams would rather not deal with these heroes at all, as they enable strategies that are difficult to counter or are simply overpowered.

Diggie (Roam)

Banned 102 times. Diggie's Time Journey (Ultimate) provides a team-wide purify and shield, completely nullifying crowd-control-heavy compositions (like those with Kaja, Arlott, or Luo Yi). He is the ultimate "fun-killer" for dive teams.

Joy (Jungle/EXP)

Banned 85 times. Joy's high mobility, CC immunity during her ultimate, and strong burst damage make her an extremely slippery and dangerous target. She can disrupt formations and pick off backliners with little counterplay.

Mathilda (Roam)

Banned 77 times and picked 65 times (2nd most contested). Her ability to grant allies a free "flicker" with Guiding Wind enables incredible team mobility, ganks, and escapes. Her ultimate also provides a reliable lock-down.

The Situational Aces

Heroes with staggering win rates but lower pick rates. They weren't picked every game, but when they were (likely in specific "counter" situations), they dominated.

Kaja (Roam/EXP)

A massive 71.4% WR over 14 games. Kaja's Divine Judgment is the ultimate pick-off tool, a suppression that pulls an enemy out of position. He is the perfect counter to high-mobility heroes (like Nolan, Joy, Claude) that lack a purify.

Luo Yi (Mid)

A 66.7% WR over 36 games (and 73 bans). Luo Yi provides huge AoE magic damage and CC. Her ultimate, Diversion, allows for instant team-wide rotations, creating ganks and objective pressures out of nowhere.

Khaleed (EXP Lane)

A 63.6% WR over 11 games. A lane bully with incredible sustain, Khaleed can dominate the early game. His ultimate provides a great engagement tool, and he thrives in extended fights where his self-heal shines.

Meta Counter Strategies

No lineup is unbeatable. Here are the common strategies and hero archetypes used to counter the "Meta Standard" composition.

Strategy 1: The "Anti-Dive" / "Purify" Comp

Goal: To survive the burst and CC from heroes like Nolan, Arlott, and Mathilda.

How it works: This lineup is built around negating the enemy's engagement. By picking a team-wide purify, you render their entire "all-in" strategy useless, leaving them overextended and vulnerable.

Core Heroes: Diggie (Roam), Minotaur (Roam)

Strategy 2: The "Pick-Off" Comp

Goal: To punish the high-mobility meta heroes (Nolan, Claude) who often rely on dashes instead of Purify.

How it works: This strategy uses hard, single-target suppression CC to catch a key target out of position. One successful pick-off on the enemy jungler or marksman can lead to a free Lord or a won teamfight.

Core Heroes: Kaja (Roam/EXP), Franco (Roam)

Strategy 3: The "Front-to-Back" Tank

Goal: To out-sustain the enemy burst and win extended teamfights.

How it works: Instead of trying to match the enemy's mobility, this lineup focuses on durability and sustained damage. A tanky jungler and EXP laner can absorb the initial burst and then turn the fight, grinding the enemy down. This works well against assassin-heavy teams.

Core Heroes: Baxia (Jungle), Khaleed (EXP), Fredrinn (Jungle/EXP)
